Privacy Policy

Your Privacy is Our Priority

Protecting your data while connecting space enthusiasts worldwide

Last updated: July 9, 2025

Our Commitment

Vyomarr ("we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your data when you visit our website and use our services.

1
General Principles

At Vyomarr, we believe that privacy is a fundamental right. Our data protection framework is built upon six core principles that guide every decision we make regarding your personal information. These principles ensure that your data is handled with the utmost care and respect throughout its entire lifecycle with us.

Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency

We process personal data lawfully, fairly, and transparently. Every data processing activity we undertake has a clear legal basis, and we are always transparent about what data we collect and how we use it. You will never find hidden data collection practices or unclear terms in our policies.

Purpose Limitation

Data is collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes and not further processed in a manner incompatible with those purposes. When we collect your information, we tell you exactly why we need it and stick to that purpose. We don't repurpose your data for activities you haven't agreed to.

Data Minimization

We only collect data that is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary for the purposes for which it is processed. This means we don't collect excessive information "just in case" we might need it later. Every piece of data we request serves a specific, justifiable purpose.

Accuracy

We take reasonable steps to ensure that personal data is accurate and, where necessary, kept up to date. We provide you with tools to update your information and regularly review our data to ensure its accuracy. Inaccurate data is promptly corrected or deleted.

Storage Limitation

Data is kept in a form which permits identification of data subjects for no longer than necessary. We have clear retention schedules and automatically delete or anonymize data when it's no longer needed. This ensures your personal information doesn't linger in our systems indefinitely.

Integrity and Confidentiality

We process data securely, protecting against unauthorized or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage using appropriate technical and organizational measures. Our security measures are continuously updated to meet the latest standards and threats.

2
Information We Collect

Understanding what information we collect is crucial for maintaining transparency in our relationship with you. We categorize the information we collect into three main types, each serving specific purposes in delivering and improving our services.

Personal Information

This includes any information that can directly identify you as an individual. When you create an account with Vyomarr, subscribe to our newsletter, or contact our support team, you voluntarily provide us with personal details such as your name, email address, phone number, and postal address. We also collect professional information if you're representing an organization, including your job title and company name. This information is essential for creating your personalized experience on our platform and enabling us to communicate with you effectively about our services, updates, and space-related content that matches your interests.

Usage Data and Analytics

Every time you interact with our website or mobile application, we automatically collect technical information about your visit. This includes your IP address, which helps us understand your general location and ensure our services work properly in your region. We also gather information about your device, such as the type of browser you're using, your operating system, screen resolution, and device identifiers. Additionally, we track your browsing patterns, including which pages you visit, how long you spend on each section, what content you engage with most, and the sequence of your actions on our platform. This data helps us understand how users navigate our site, identify popular content, and optimize the user experience.

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We employ various technologies to enhance your browsing experience and gather insights about how our services are used. Cookies are small text files stored on your device that remember your preferences, login status, and browsing history. We use essential cookies that are necessary for the website to function properly, such as maintaining your login session and remembering your language preferences. Performance cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting anonymous information about page views, traffic sources, and user behavior patterns. We also use functionality cookies to remember your choices and provide enhanced, personalized features. These technologies enable us to offer you a seamless, personalized experience while continuously improving our services based on user behavior insights.

3
How We Use Your Information

Your information is the foundation that allows us to provide you with exceptional service and continuously improve your experience with Vyomarr. We use your data responsibly and strategically across several key areas of our operations.

Service Provision and Maintenance

Your personal information enables us to create and maintain your account, process your requests, and deliver the core features of our platform. We use your contact details to send important service notifications, account updates, and technical announcements. Your usage patterns help us ensure our systems are running smoothly and identify any technical issues that might affect your experience. When you contact our support team, we use your information to provide personalized assistance and resolve any problems you encounter.

Personalization and Enhancement

We analyze your interactions with our platform to understand your interests and preferences, allowing us to customize your experience with relevant content, features, and recommendations. Your browsing history and engagement patterns help us suggest space-related articles, events, and community discussions that align with your interests. We also use this information to optimize our website's performance, improve navigation, and develop new features that address our users' needs and preferences.

Communication and Community Building

We use your contact information to send you newsletters, updates about new features, and notifications about community activities that might interest you. Our communication strategy is designed to keep you engaged with the space enthusiast community while respecting your preferences. You can always control what types of communications you receive from us and can opt out at any time.

Legal Compliance and Security

We process your information to comply with applicable laws, regulations, and legal requests from authorities. This includes maintaining records for tax purposes, responding to legal inquiries, and cooperating with law enforcement when required. We also use your data to protect the security and integrity of our services, prevent fraud, detect suspicious activities, and ensure the safety of all our users.

4
Legal Basis for Processing

Every piece of personal data we process must have a solid legal foundation. We ensure that all our data processing activities are legally justified and transparent to you.

Your Explicit Consent

In many cases, we process your personal data because you have given us clear, unambiguous consent to do so. This includes when you sign up for our newsletter, agree to receive marketing communications, or consent to cookies on our website. Your consent is freely given, specific, informed, and can be withdrawn at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.

Contract Performance

When you use our services, we enter into a contractual relationship with you. Processing your personal data is necessary to fulfill our obligations under this contract, such as providing you with access to our platform, maintaining your account, and delivering the services you've requested. This includes processing payment information, managing your subscription, and providing customer support.

Legal Obligations

Sometimes we must process your personal data to comply with legal requirements. This includes maintaining certain records for tax and accounting purposes, responding to valid legal requests from authorities, complying with court orders, and meeting regulatory requirements in the jurisdictions where we operate.

Legitimate Interests

We may process your personal data when it's necessary for our legitimate business interests, provided these interests don't override your fundamental rights and freedoms. Our legitimate interests include improving our services, ensuring network security, preventing fraud, conducting analytics to understand user behavior, and marketing our services to existing customers. We always conduct a careful balancing test to ensure your privacy rights are protected.

5
Data Sharing and Disclosure
We do not sell your personal information to third parties, ever.

Your trust is paramount to us, and we are committed to being transparent about when and why we might share your information with others. Any sharing of your data is done with strict safeguards and only when necessary.

Trusted Service Providers

We work with carefully selected third-party service providers who help us deliver and improve our services. These partners include cloud hosting providers who store our data securely, email service providers who help us communicate with you, analytics companies that help us understand user behavior, and payment processors who handle transactions securely. All these partners are bound by strict confidentiality agreements and are only permitted to use your data for the specific services they provide to us. We regularly audit these relationships to ensure your data remains protected.

Legal Requirements and Authorities

In rare circumstances, we may be legally required to disclose your information to government authorities, law enforcement agencies, or courts. This only happens when we receive a valid legal request, such as a court order, subpoena, or when disclosure is necessary to comply with applicable laws. We carefully review all such requests to ensure they are legitimate and will challenge overly broad or inappropriate requests when possible. We will notify you about such disclosures when legally permitted to do so.

Business Transactions

In the unlikely event that Vyomarr is involved in a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your personal data may be transferred as part of that transaction. However, we will ensure that any acquiring party is bound by the same privacy commitments outlined in this policy. We will provide you with advance notice of any such transfer and inform you of any changes to how your data will be handled. You will have the opportunity to delete your account before any such transfer if you choose not to continue with the new entity.

6
Data Security

Protecting your personal information is one of our highest priorities. We implement a comprehensive security framework that includes both technical and organizational measures to safeguard your data against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction.

Our technical security measures include industry-standard encryption for data both in transit and at rest, secure server configurations, regular security updates and patches, multi-factor authentication for our internal systems, and continuous monitoring for suspicious activities. We use advanced firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and employ security professionals who regularly assess and improve our defenses.

From an organizational standpoint, we maintain strict access controls ensuring that only authorized personnel can access your data on a need-to-know basis. All our employees undergo comprehensive privacy and security training, and we conduct regular security audits and penetration testing to identify and address potential vulnerabilities.

However, we want to be transparent with you: no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is completely secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal data and continuously invest in improving our security measures, we cannot guarantee absolute security. We encourage you to also take steps to protect your account, such as using strong, unique passwords and keeping your login credentials confidential.

7
Data Retention

We believe in retaining your personal data only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, comply with legal requirements, and protect our legitimate business interests. Our retention approach is systematic and considers various factors to determine appropriate retention periods.

For account information, we retain your personal data as long as your account remains active and for a reasonable period afterward to accommodate potential account reactivation. When you delete your account, we initiate a deletion process, though some information may be retained for a limited time to comply with legal obligations or resolve disputes.

Technical and usage data is typically retained for shorter periods, usually between 12-24 months, depending on the type of data and its purpose. This includes server logs, analytics data, and similar technical information. Marketing and communication data is retained until you unsubscribe from our communications or delete your account.

Certain types of data may need to be retained longer due to legal requirements, such as financial records for tax purposes or information related to legal proceedings. In all cases, once the retention period expires, we securely delete or anonymize the data so it can no longer be associated with you personally.

8
Your Rights

You have significant control over your personal data, and we're committed to helping you exercise your rights effectively. The specific rights available to you may vary depending on your location and applicable privacy laws, but generally include the following comprehensive set of protections.

Right to Access Your Information

You have the right to know what personal information we hold about you and to receive a copy of this data. When you make an access request, we'll provide you with details about what data we collect, how we use it, who we share it with, and how long we retain it. We'll also provide you with a copy of your personal data in a structured, commonly used format.

Right to Correct and Update

If you notice that any of your personal information is inaccurate, incomplete, or outdated, you have the right to have it corrected or updated. You can make most corrections yourself through your account settings, or you can contact us to make changes on your behalf. We'll respond promptly to correction requests and update our records accordingly.

Right to Deletion ("Right to be Forgotten")

You can request that we delete your personal data in certain circumstances, such as when the data is no longer necessary for the original purpose, you withdraw your consent, or the data has been unlawfully processed. When you delete your account, we'll remove your personal information from our active systems, though some data may be retained for legal compliance purposes.

Right to Restrict or Object to Processing

You can request that we limit how we use your personal data or object to certain types of processing. For example, you might object to processing for marketing purposes or request that we temporarily restrict processing while we resolve a dispute about the accuracy of your data. We'll honor these requests except where we have compelling legitimate grounds to continue processing.

Right to Data Portability

Where technically feasible, you have the right to receive your personal data in a portable format and to transmit it to another service provider. This helps ensure you're not locked into our services and can easily move your data if you choose to use alternative platforms.

Right to Withdraw Consent

Where our processing is based on your consent, you can withdraw that consent at any time. This includes unsubscribing from marketing emails, changing your cookie preferences, or withdrawing consent for specific data processing activities. Withdrawing consent won't affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.

Right to Lodge a Complaint

If you believe your rights have been violated or that we are not handling your personal data in accordance with applicable laws, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the relevant data protection authority in your jurisdiction. We encourage you to contact us first so we can address your concerns directly, but you are free to escalate the matter to the authorities if needed.

If you have any questions or concerns about your rights or our privacy practices, please contact us at support@vyomarr.com.
